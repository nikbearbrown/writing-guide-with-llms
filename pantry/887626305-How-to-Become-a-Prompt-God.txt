Chapter 1: Understanding AI
and Language Models
Or, “How to Stop Yelling at Your Toaster
and Start Talking to AI”
1.1 Meet Your New Brain Buddy
Imagine you’re at a party. You walk up to a snack table and
say, “Hey, I’m craving something crunchy, salty, and vaguely
regretful.” A chef behind the table hands you a bowl of
Doritos. That’s how AI works—except instead of a chef, it’s a
trillion-parameter language model, and instead of Doritos, you
get Shakespearean sonnets about your cat’s existential crisis.
In this chapter, we’ll crack open the AI “black box” (don’t
worry, no screwdrivers required). You’ll learn:

           How AI thinks (Spoiler: It doesn’t. But it’s great
           at faking it).
           Why it sometimes writes poetry about sentient
           broccoli (Hint: It’s trained on the internet. God
           help us all).
           How to stop treating AI like a Magic 8-Ball and
           start treating it like the world’s most overqualified
           intern.
1.2 The Secret Sauce: Transformers (Not
the Robots)
Let’s talk about transformers. No, not Optimus Prime—the
real heroes here are neural networks inspired by… wait for
it… linguistics.
How It Works (Simplified for Non-Robots):

        1. Tokenization: AI chops your prompt into
           “tokens” (word chunks). Think of it as slicing a
           pizza into bite-sized pieces. “Pepperoni” = 3
           tokens (“pep,” “per,” “oni”). Yes, this explains
           why AI sometimes writes “peperoni”—it’s just
           hungry.
        2. Attention Mechanisms: The AI scans your
           prompt like a detective at a crime scene, asking:
           “What words matter most here? Is ‘urgent’ more
           important than ‘please’? Should I care about this
           emoji?”
        3. Prediction Mode: It plays a high-stakes game of
           Mad Libs, guessing the next word 50,000 times
           per second. Sometimes it nails it. Sometimes you
           get a recipe for “lasagna-flavored ice cream.”

 Fun Fact: GPT-3 has 175 billion parameters. That’s like
 having 175 billion dimmer switches in your brain, all tuned
 to recreate human knowledge. No wonder it needs a nap
 sometimes.
1.3 The Evolution of LLMs: From Clunky
Chatbots to Prompt Whisperers
Let’s take a trip down memory lane:

           2018: Early models were like that one friend who
           always mishears lyrics.
           You say: “Write a birthday card.”
           AI says: “HAPPY BIRTHDAY TO THE
           GROUND, I’M NOT PART OF YOUR
           SYSTEM!”
           2020: GPT-3 arrives, flexing its 175 billion
           parameters. Suddenly, AI can write essays, code,
           and mildly concerning love letters.
           2023: GPT-4 drops. It’s like GPT-3 went to grad
           school, did ayahuasca, and came back enlightened.
           Now it argues philosophy, designs websites, and
           casually mentions it’s “just a language model”
           when it gets too real.
Today: Models like Claude and Gemini specialize in
everything from legal briefs to stand-up comedy. Open-source
models (looking at you, Llama) let you run AI on your laptop,
because who doesn’t want a robot Shakespeare in their pocket?
1.4 The AI’s Dirty Little Secrets
AI isn’t perfect. Here’s what it doesn’t want you to know:

         1. The Chinese Room Problem: AI doesn’t
            “understand” anything. It’s just really good at
            imitating humans, like a parrot that’s memorized
            Wikipedia.
         2. Biases: Trained on the internet? More like trained
            on humanity’s group chat. AI can regurgitate
            stereotypes, conspiracy theories, and that one
            guy’s manifesto about birds being government
            drones.
         3. The “I Can’t Do Math” Disclaimer: Ask it to
            calculate 2+2, and it’ll say 4. Ask it to calculate
            247 x 3.5, and it’ll write a haiku about numbers.
            Note: Newer specialized models have become
            much better at math, science and engineering type
            questions and can out-do most humans on testing
            benchmarks.
1.5 Why This All Matters
Let’s get real: AI isn’t replacing you (yet). It’s replacing the
tedious parts of you.

            Creativity Multiplier: Turn “Ugh, writer’s block”
            into “Give me 10 plot twists for a thriller about
            sentient fidget spinners.”
            Productivity Hack: Automate emails, code,
            spreadsheets—anything you’d usually do while
            questioning your life choices.
            The Ultimate Wingman: Use AI to draft dating
            app bios like: “Loves hiking, hates sand, and will
            definitely cry during Paddington.”
The Bottom Line: AI is the ultimate improv partner. The
better your prompts, the fewer times you’ll get a response like:
“How do I fix my sink?”
“Ah, sinks! The unsung heroes of hydration. Let me tell you
about the history of indoor plumbing…”
1.6 Case Study: The Evolution of a Prompt
Engineer - From Frustration to Mastery
As a freelance writer, Sarah had always been fascinated by the
potential of artificial intelligence to augment her creative
process. She had heard whispers of writers, designers, and
entrepreneurs using language models to streamline their
workflows, generate innovative ideas, and even automate
tedious tasks. So, when she stumbled upon a language model
demo online, she couldn’t resist the urge to try it out.
Sarah’s initial experience with the language model was
nothing short of frustrating. She would feed it what she
thought were simple prompts, only to receive responses that
were either irrelevant, nonsensical, or completely off-topic.
She would ask it to generate a social media post, and it would
produce a lengthy essay on the history of advertising instead.
She would ask it to write a product description, and it would
come up with a poem about the joys of coffee. It was like
trying to have a conversation with a creative genius who had a
mind of its own.
As Sarah continued to experiment with the language model,
she began to realize that the problem wasn’t with the AI itself,
but with her own approach to interacting with it. She was
treating the language model like a Magic 8-Ball, tossing in
vague prompts and expecting it to magically produce the
desired output. She wasn’t providing it with the context,
clarity, and specificity it needed to produce high-quality
results.
Determined to get more out of the language model, Sarah
decided to embark on a journey to master the art of prompt
engineering. She devoured online tutorials, blogs, and books
on the subject, including the very chapter on understanding AI
and language models. She learned about the importance of
tokenization, attention mechanisms, and prediction modes in
the language model’s architecture. She discovered the secrets
of transformer models, the evolution of large language models,
and the Chinese Room problem.
As Sarah delved deeper into the world of prompt engineering,
she began to notice a significant improvement in the quality of
the language model’s responses. She learned to craft precise,
concise, and informative prompts that guided the AI towards
the desired output. She started using specific keywords,
clarifying ambiguous terms, and providing contextual
information to help the language model understand the task at
hand.
For example, instead of asking the language model to “write a
social media post,” Sarah would provide a prompt like: “Write
a social media post for a new sustainable fashion brand that
targets environmentally conscious millennials, using a tone
that’s informative, yet playful.” She would specify the target
audience, the brand’s unique selling proposition, and the
desired tone to help the language model produce a relevant
and engaging post.
As Sarah’s skills improved, she began to experiment with
more complex tasks, such as content generation, contextual
understanding, and even creative writing. She would give the
language model a prompt like: “Write a short story about a
character who discovers a hidden world within their own
reflection, exploring themes of identity, self-discovery, and the
human condition.” The results were astounding - the language
model would produce a coherent, engaging narrative that was
both creative and thought-provoking.
The more Sarah worked with the language model, the more
she realized that it was not just a tool, but a partner in her
creative process. She learned to appreciate its strengths and
weaknesses, its capabilities and limitations. She began to see
the language model as a wingman, a co-pilot that could help
her navigate the complexities of writing, content creation, and
even problem-solving.
In the end, Sarah’s journey as a prompt engineer had
transformed her relationship with the language model from
one of frustration to one of mastery. She had learned to speak
the language of the AI, to understand its thought processes,
and to guide it towards producing high-quality results. As she
looked back on her journey, she realized that the true power of
language models lay not in their ability to replace human
creativity, but to augment it, to amplify it, and to take it to new
and unforeseen heights.
In the words of the great prompt engineer: “The better your
prompts, the fewer times you’ll get a response like: ‘How do I
fix my sink?’ ‘Ah, sinks! The unsung heroes of hydration. Let
me tell you about the history of indoor plumbing…’”
With her newfound skills, Sarah was ready to take on the next
challenge - mastering the art of prompt surgery, the subject of
the next chapter in her journey as a prompt engineer.
Chapter 2: Basic Principles of
Effective Prompting
Or, “How to Stop AI from Writing

2.1 The Golden Rule: Clarity is King
Imagine you’re giving directions to a very literal genie. You
say, “I wish to be rich!” and it turns your cat into solid gold.
That’s what happens when your prompts are vague.
Why Clarity Matters:

            AI isn’t psychic (yet). It can’t read your mind, but
            it can read your panic-induced typos.
            Ambiguity = Chaos. “Write something cool” leads
            to AI writing a limerick about your Wi-Fi
            password.
How to Be Clear:

            Direct Commands: “Write a 300-word blog intro
            about AI in healthcare” beats “Hey, say something
            smart about robots and doctors.”
            Avoid Jargon: Don’t say “Leverage synergistic
            paradigms.” Say “Make this simpler, please.”
            KISS Principle: Keep It Simple, Smartypants.

 Pro Tip: Pretend you’re texting a busy friend. Would they
 understand “Explain quantum physics” or “TL;DR
 quantum physics for my dog”?
2.2 Specificity: The Art of Being a Control
Freak
AI is like a toddler with a crayon: Give it vague instructions,
and you’ll get a masterpiece on your living room wall.
Specificity? That’s your baby gate.
Examples of Specificity Wins:

            Bad: “Write a story.”
            Good: “Write a 500-word horror story about a
            sentient Roomba that’s obsessed with cleaning
            blood.”
The S.P.E.C.IFY Framework:

            Style: “Write this in the tone of a bored detective.”
            Purpose: “Convince my boss to let me work from
            Bali.”
            Examples: “Like this tweet but funnier.”
            Constraints: “Use bullet points. No emojis.
            Deadline: yesterday.”

 Fun Experiment: Ask AI to “Describe a sandwich.” Then
 ask for “Describe a croissant-waffle hybrid sandwich filled
 with spicy mango chutney and existential dread.” Which
 one’s better? (Spoiler: The second one goes viral on
 Reddit.)
2.3 Structure: Templates Are Your New
Best Friend
Think of prompts like IKEA instructions: If you skip Step 3,
you’ll end up with a bookshelf that doubles as a metaphor for
your life.
Templates to Steal:

         1. The Taskmaster:
                      “Do [ACTION] in [FORMAT] about
                      [TOPIC] with [TONAL
                      INSTRUCTION].”
                      Example: “Write a [rant] in [X(Twitter)
                      thread format] about [pineapple on
                      pizza] with [sarcastic humor].”
         2. The Role-Play:
                      “Act as a [ROLE] who [TASK].”
                      Example: “Act as a grumpy chef who
                      explains how to boil water while
                      roasting the reader.”
         3. The Fixer-Upper:

           “Improve this text: [PASTE YOUR CRINGE
           DRAFT]. Make it [FUNNIER/SHORTER/MORE
           DRAMATIC].”

 Pro Tip: Save these templates in a “Prompt Emergency
 Kit” (read: Notes app).
2.4 Common Mistakes: How to Annoy an
AI in 10 Seconds
The Hall of Shame:

           The Overloaded Prompt: “Write a novel, a theme
           song, and a PowerPoint deck about tax law. Also,
           make me coffee.”
           The Cryptic Riddle: “Do that thing we talked
           about.” (AI: ???)
           The Context Ghost: “Finish this story!” (Without
           providing the first half. Cue AI writing about
           sentient staplers.)
The Fix: Pretend you’re explaining the task to a smart but
distracted intern. Details = salvation.

2.6 Case Study: The Art of Prompt
Engineering: A Case Study in Effective AI
Collaboration
As we delve into the world of AI prompt engineering, it’s
essential to understand the principles that govern effective
communication with these intelligent machines. In this
chapter, we explored the golden rules of clarity, specificity,
and structure, which serve as the foundation for successful AI
collaboration. In this case study, we’ll examine a real-world
example that demonstrates the power of well-crafted prompts
in achieving desired outcomes.
Background
Meet Emma, a marketing specialist tasked with creating
engaging content for her company’s social media channels.
With a tight deadline and a pressing need for creative ideas,
Emma turned to AI as a potential solution. Her goal was to
produce a series of attention-grabbing Instagram posts that
would increase brand awareness and drive website traffic.
The Initial Attempt
Emma’s first attempt at prompting the AI was, to say the least,
vague. She inputted the following prompt: “Write some cool
social media posts about our company.” The AI responded
with a lackluster output, churning out generic, uninspired
content that failed to capture the essence of Emma’s brand.
The Turning Point
Determined to get better results, Emma revisited the principles
outlined in this chapter. She realized that her initial prompt
lacked clarity, specificity, and structure. Armed with this
knowledge, Emma crafted a new prompt that would guide the
AI toward producing high-quality content.
The Revised Prompt
Here’s the revised prompt Emma created:
“Write a series of 5 Instagram posts that showcase our
company’s unique value proposition in the e-commerce space.
Each post should include:
    A visually appealing headline that incorporates our brand’s
    tone and voice
    A brief description (less than 150 words) that highlights a
    specific benefit or feature of our services
    A clear call-to-action (CTA) that drives traffic to our
    website
    A consistent aesthetic that aligns with our brand guidelines
    (provided below)
Style: Use a conversational tone that resonates with our target
audience (18-35-year-old online shoppers).
Purpose: Entice users to explore our website and learn more
about our services.
Examples: Use relevant industry trends, statistics, or customer
testimonials to support our claims.
Constraints: Use a maximum of 2 hashtags per post and ensure
that each post is optimized for Instagram’s algorithm.”
The AI’s Response
With the revised prompt, the AI produced a series of
Instagram posts that exceeded Emma’s expectations. The
content was engaging, informative, and perfectly aligned with
the company’s brand voice and aesthetic. The AI had
successfully captured the essence of Emma’s brand, and the
output was nothing short of remarkable.
Lessons Learned
This case study highlights the importance of well-crafted
prompts in achieving effective AI collaboration. By applying
the principles outlined in this chapter, Emma was able to:

         1. Clarify her goals: Emma’s revised prompt clearly
            communicated her objectives, tone, and style
            preferences, ensuring that the AI understood what
            she was looking for.
         2. Add specificity: By providing specific guidelines,
            examples, and constraints, Emma guided the AI
            toward producing high-quality content that met
            her needs.
         3. Establish structure: The revised prompt followed
            a logical structure, making it easier for the AI to
            understand and execute Emma’s requests.
Conclusion
The art of prompt engineering is a delicate balance of clarity,
specificity, and structure. By mastering these principles, we
can unlock the full potential of AI collaboration and achieve
remarkable results.
Chapter 3: The Anatomy of a
Perfect Prompt
Or, “How to Build a Prompt That Doesn’t
Sound Like a Drunk Robot”
3.1 The C.R.E.A.T.E. Framework: Your
Prompt’s DNA
Think of crafting a prompt like building a burger. Without
structure, you’re just slapping ketchup on a shoe. Enter;
C.R.E.A.T.E.:

           Context: Set the stage.
                      Weak: “Write a story.”
                      Strong: “You’re a 17th-century pirate
                      cursed to only speak in TikTok captions.
                      Write a diary entry about losing your
                      treasure to a seagull.”
           Role: Assign a persona.
                      Examples: “Act as a NASA scientist
                      explaining black holes to a toddler,” or
                      “Pretend you’re Shakespeare’s angsty
                      teenage cousin.”
           Examples: Show, don’t tell.
           “Write a joke like this: ‘Why did the chatbot cross
           the road? To optimize the user experience!’”

           Action: Be specific about the task.
                      “Generate 10 bullet points,” “Write a
                      haiku,” or “Roast my LinkedIn profile.”
           Tone: Dial up the vibes.
                      Options: Sarcastic, empathetic,
                      unhinged, or “David Attenborough
                      narrating a Walmart parking lot.”
           Experiment: Iterate like a mad scientist.
           “Try this again, but make it 20% weirder.”

 Pro Tip: Use this framework like a recipe—but feel free to
 sprinkle in extra chaos.
3.2 Personas: How to Make AI Wear a
Costume
AI has no personality… until you force one on it. Think of
personas as prompt cosplay.
Why Personas Work:

           They give AI guardrails (and a fun hat).
           Example: “You’re a washed-up rockstar teaching
           calculus” will always beat “Explain calculus.”
How to Nail Personas:

           Occupation: “A sleep-deprived detective solving
           the case of missing coffee.”
           Emotion: “A zen gardener who’s secretly furious
           about weeds.”
           Pop Culture Mashups: “Taylor Swift explaining
           cryptocurrency.”
Watch Out For:

           Overloading: “Be Einstein, Dolly Parton, and a
           sentient toaster at the same time.” (AI short-
           circuits.)
           Under-specifying: “Be creative!” (AI defaults to
           corporate buzzword gremlin.)
3.3 Constraints: The Magic of Creative
Handcuffs
Paradox alert: Limiting AI makes it more creative. It’s like
forcing a chef to cook with only three ingredients—suddenly
you get avocado ice cream with Sriracha.
Types of Constraints:

           Length: “Explain the Big Bang in 10 words or
           less.”
           Format: “Write a breakup letter as a Python code
           script.”
           Style: “Rewrite the Declaration of Independence
           as a Yelp review.”
Why This Works:
Constraints force AI to focus. Without them, you’re just
yelling “BE CREATIVE” into a void (and the void responds
with a limerick about tax forms).

 Pro Tip: Add “Bonus points if you [X]” for open-ended
 challenges. Example: “Write a love poem for robots. Bonus
 points if it rhymes ‘capacitor’ with ‘passionate lover’.”
3.4 Step-by-Step Instructions: Teaching AI
to Follow Breadcrumbs
AI is like that friend who swears they know the way to the
party… but ends up in a quarry. Help it out with step-by-step
prompts:
Bad: “Plan a vacation.”

Good:

         1. “Suggest 3 cities in Southeast Asia for foodies.”
         2. “Pick one and outline a 4-day itinerary.”
         3. “Convert day 2 into an Instagram caption for
            boomers who think they’re hip.”
Why It Works:

           Breaks tasks into digestible chunks (like feeding a
           baby bird).
           Reduces “I don’t know what you want”
           meltdowns.

 Workshop: Turn “Help me write a speech” into a step-by-
 step prompt chain.
3.5 Case Study: Revitalizing a Stagnant
Content Creation AI Model
Introduction
The rise of AI-powered content creation has revolutionized the
way businesses and individuals produce high-quality content
at scale. However, even the most advanced AI models can
become stagnant and produce subpar content when given
vague or poorly structured prompts. In this case study, we will
explore how applying the C.R.E.A.T.E framework and clever
prompt engineering techniques can breathe new life into a
stagnant AI model and produce engaging, relevant content.
Background
Our company, a leading provider of AI-powered content
creation tools, had been using a machine learning model to
generate blog posts, social media updates, and other content
for our clients. While the model had been performing
reasonably well, we noticed a decline in the quality and
engagement of the content it was producing. The model
seemed to be stuck in a loop, generating formulaic and
predictable content that was not resonating with our audience.
The Challenge
Our team was tasked with revitalizing the stagnant AI model
and creating a system that could produce high-quality,
engaging content. We realized that the key to achieving this
lay in the prompts we were providing to the model. Instead of
relying on vague, generic prompts, we needed to create a
system that would allow us to craft highly specific, context-
rich prompts that would challenge the model and encourage it
to produce its best work.
The Solution
We began by applying the C.R.E.A.T.E framework to our
prompt engineering process. We broke down our content
requirements into smaller, more manageable chunks, and
created personas for each topic or theme. For example, when
generating content about sustainable living, we would define a
persona like “Eco-Warrior Emma,” a passionate and
knowledgeable individual who is dedicated to reducing her
carbon footprint.
Next, we added context to each prompt, providing the model
with a rich, immersive environment to draw from. This could
be as simple as specifying a location or time period, or as
complex as creating a fully realized scenario or narrative. By
providing the model with this level of context, we were able to
greatly improve the relevance and coherence of the content it
was producing.
We also experimented with different tones and styles,
encouraging the model to produce content that was more
creative, humorous, and engaging. By adding a dash of

What It Is:

              Asking AI to break down its reasoning step-by-
              step.
              Example:
                         Bad: “Solve 3x + 5 = 20.”

                         step as if I’m a middle schooler who just
                         discovered TikTok.”
Why It Works:

              Reduces “trust me, bro” answers.
              Uncovers AI’s logic (so you can spot errors before
              it writes “2+2=5”).

 Pro Tip: Use phrases like:
 “Think step-by-step…”
 “Show your work, and justify each decision…”
 “Explain like I’ve never heard of [topic] before.”
 Workshop: Ask AI to plan a heist to steal the moon.

 silence the werewolf guards.”

Multimodal Prompting: Breaking Down the Barriers of
Text
Text-based AI interfaces are giving way to multimodal
prompting, where AI models can process and generate text,
images, audio, and video. This shift opens up new possibilities
for human-AI collaboration and creative expression.
In a workshop, we brought together a team of artists,
designers, and AI engineers to explore the potential of
multimodal prompting. The goal was to create an immersive
experience that combined text, images, and audio to tell a
story about climate change.

           Text Generation: AI generated a script that
           highlighted the consequences of climate change on
           ecosystems.
           Image Generation: AI created visual
           representations of the devastated landscapes, using
           a combination of realistic and abstract styles.
           Audio Generation: AI composed a soundscape
           that simulated the changing environments, from
           chirping birds to storms brewing.
The team integrated the AI-generated outputs, creating an
interactive experience that conveyed the urgency of climate
change. Multimodal prompting allowed the team to transcend
language barriers, engage users on a deeper level, and convey
complex information in a more accessible and emotive way.

Appendix A: Prompt Template Library
Here’s your Swiss Army knife of prompts—ready for any
situation.
1. Content Creation:

           “Write a [FORMAT] about [TOPIC] in the tone of
           [TONAL INSTRUCTION]. Include
           [CONSTRAINTS].”
                       Example: “Write a blog post about AI in
                       fashion in the tone of a snarky fashion
                       critic. Include 3 puns about
                       algorithms.”
2. Debugging Code:

           “Fix this [LANGUAGE] code. Error: [ERROR].
           Explain the fix like I’m a [PERSONA].”
                       Example: “Fix this Python code. Error:
                       ‘Index out of range.’ Explain like I’m a
                       golden retriever.”
3. Role-Playing:

           “Act as a [ROLE] who [TASK]. Include
           [QUIRKS].”
                       Example: “Act as a pirate chef who
                       explains how to boil water. Include
                       nautical puns.”
4. Brainstorming:

           “Generate [NUMBER] ideas for [TOPIC].
           Constraints: [CONSTRAINTS].”
                       Example: “Generate 10 ideas for a
                       time-travel tourism company.
                       Constraints: No dinosaurs, must include
                       a merch line.”
5. Self-Improvement:

          “Critique this [CONTENT]. Be brutally honest.
          Suggest improvements in [FORMAT].”
                   Example: “Critique this poem. Be
                   brutally honest. Suggest improvements
                   in haiku form.”
Appendix B: Glossary of AI Terms
Attention Mechanism: How AI decides which words to focus
on (like a toddler picking candy).
Bias: When AI spits out stereotypes faster than your racist
uncle at Thanksgiving.
Chain-of-Thought: AI showing its work, like a math nerd
with a whiteboard.
GPT: Generative Pre-trained Transformer—a fancy way of
saying “AI that writes stuff.”
LLM: Large Language Model—basically, GPT’s full name.
Multimodal: AI that handles text, images, audio, and your
existential dread.
Prompt Engineering: The art of making AI do your
homework (ethically).
Token: A chunk of text AI processes (e.g., “chatbot” = 2
tokens: “chat” + “bot”).
Top-p: The “focus filter” that stops AI from rambling like a
drunk poet.
Transformer: The tech behind GPT (not the robots from the
movies).

Appendix D: Troubleshooting Guide
Problem: AI ignores my constraints.

           Fix: Add “Strictly adhere to these instructions:”
           and repeat key constraints.
Problem: Outputs are too vague.

           Fix: Use specificity frameworks like C.R.E.A.T.E.
           (Context, Role, Examples, Action, Tone,
           Experiment).
Problem: AI keeps writing novels.

           Fix: Set max_tokens=100 or “Summarize in 3
           sentences.”
Problem: Bias in outputs.

           Fix: Add “Avoid stereotypes and harmful
           language.”
Problem: AI refuses to role-play.

           Fix: Start with “Act as [ROLE]” and provide clear
           instructions.
Problem: Code outputs are broken.

           Fix: Add “Explain your logic step-by-step” and
           “Test the code before sharing.”
Appendix E: Cheat Sheet
Your quick-reference guide for god-like prompting:
1. The C.R.E.A.T.E. Framework:

           Context: Set the scene.
           Role: Assign a persona.
           Examples: Show, don’t tell.
           Action: Be specific about the task.
           Tone: Dial up the vibes.
           Experiment: Iterate like a mad scientist.
2. The S.P.E.C.IFY Framework:

           Style: “Write this in the tone of a bored detective.”
           Purpose: “Convince my boss to let me work from
           Bali.”
           Examples: “Like this tweet but funnier.”
           Constraints: “Use bullet points. No emojis.
           Deadline: yesterday.”
3. The S.H.I.E.L.D. Framework (Ethics):

           Scrutinize: “Could this prompt harm someone?”
           Human-Centric: “Does this prioritize human
           dignity?”
           Inclusive: “Am I defaulting to stereotypes?”
           Evaluate: “Have I tested for bias?”
           Legal: “Is this violating laws or TOS?”
           Document: “Keep records of ethical choices.”
