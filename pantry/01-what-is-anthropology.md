## Analogies

- **Culture as fabric** ("pull one thread, the cloth moves") — used to explain holism — limits: implies neat weave, but real cultures have tangles, thinning patches, and seams under tension. Acknowledged in the "holism is not harmony" subsection.
- **Marriage form tracking food-getting strategy** — not strictly an analogy; a structural correspondence. Reusable as: case for material-base shaping ideational superstructure without invoking Marx by name.
- **Mobile money cascade → infant mortality** — works as a model for how holism finds *unobvious* connections; the connection wasn't in the public-health silo because nobody was looking there.
- **Skin color as continuous spectrum (Kinshasa → Tromsø road trip)** — used to explain why race is not biologically discrete — limits: the traveler's gradual shift is too smooth in places (recent migration produces sharp local discontinuities); the analogy still holds for the *deep historical* distribution.

## Etymologies that did work

- **anthropology** — Greek *anthropos* (human) + *logia* (study) — implicit; not used explicitly but available
- **ethnocentrism** — Greek *ethnos* (a people) + Latin *centrum* (the center) — used in §3, helps the "egocentrism : ethnocentrism" analogy land
- **holism** — Greek *holos* (whole) — used twice; the second use deepens by adding "the parts of a culture are not a set of independent modules but a connected fabric"
- **kula ring** — not etymologized; could be in a future chapter on exchange

## Trade-offs named

- **Holism** optimizes for *unexpected connections* at the cost of *clean falsification*. (Concept 1)
- **Cultural relativism** optimizes for *seeing the system as it is* at the cost of *the speed and force of moral response*. (Concept 2)
- **Four-field structure** optimizes for *integrated answers to large questions* at the cost of *deep specialization*. (Concept 3)
- **Collaborative ethnography** optimizes for *accuracy and ethical accountability* at the cost of *the researcher's claim to detached authority*. (Concept 3, end)

These four are reusable trade-off frames for any methodology chapter.
